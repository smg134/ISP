The Ballad of Aunt Jemima

An original work by Tanner Loeppky 

Oh Aunty J., you entrance me so,
Sweet smell of your pancakes through my windows will flow.
Nothing but your sweet buttery flavor,
Can possibly quell my ravenous behavior.

For when I am with you I can't help but crave,
The sweet syrupy taste to me you once gave.
Together we feasted deep into the night,
Stirring and flipping and frying just right.

Oh sweet Jemima you're all that I need,
Your callused hands skillfully forming my creed.
Your leathery skin, oh so delightful,
When others look at you I become oh so spiteful.

But there is more to you, than simply your craft,
Anyone who cannot see past this is daft.
A kind, caring soul is all that you are,
A shining beacon of hope, seen from afar.

Thank you, Aunty for all that you've given,
Your delectable breakfasts keeping me driven.
Without you my mornings would be in disarray,
So to your brand name I will continue to pray.
